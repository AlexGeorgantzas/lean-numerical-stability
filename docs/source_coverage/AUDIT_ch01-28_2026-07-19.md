## Closure standard

The audit applies the project skill's core-mode rules:

- every selected named theorem, lemma, corollary, algorithm, numbered equation,
  and precise central prose claim needs a compiled source-strength theorem;
- a target-bearing error budget, residual, readiness record, representability
  fact, or output contract does not count as the producer of that fact;
- an implementation claim needs a bridge from the literal rounded execution;
- natural model-validity and algorithm-completion domains remain explicit;
- an unparameterized `O(...)`, undefined approximation relation, qualitative
  observation, empirical table, or unspecified algorithm is
  `DEFER-MISSING-PRECISE-STATEMENT`, not an invitation to invent a theorem;
- a counterexample to a proof step does not close the printed theorem, whereas
  a counterexample to the printed statement together with the strongest
  faithful correction is a terminal `SOURCE-DISCREPANCY` result; and
- optional Problems, visual-only figures/tables, editorial prose, and empirical
  outputs do not gate core mode, but they remain inventoried.

`PASS` therefore means that every *precise selected* row is terminal under
those rules.  It does not mean that qualitative prose or optional exercises
were converted into arbitrary formal statements.
