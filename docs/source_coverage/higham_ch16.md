## Hidden-Hypothesis Summary

- The existing proved square theorems assume explicit Frobenius-norm bounds, `SepLowerBound`, nonzero norm-squared hypotheses where needed, and supplied linearized equations. These are domain/reused-theorem assumptions, not replacements for the target equation.
- `sylvesterSepInf` records the exact infimum object for `sep(A,B)`; `SepLowerBound` is now equivalent to a positive lower bound of that infimum when the feasible ratio set is nonempty, and the feasible set is proved nonempty in positive dimension. Rows needing an attained minimum remain open until minimizer existence is proved.
- `sylvesterBackwardErrorInf` records the backward-error eta quantity as an infimum of nonnegative feasible `IsBackwardError` certificates. The two-sided xi bridge is proved for this infimum model; no attained-minimum theorem is claimed.
- `sylvester_schur_transform_identity`, `sylvester_schur_transform_solution_iff`, and `existsUnique_isSylvesterSolutionRect_schurDiagonal` assume supplied factorizations and orthogonality; they are not proofs of Schur decomposition existence, triangular/quasi-triangular solvability, or floating-point stability.
- `SylvesterPsiFirstOrderBound` is an explicit condition-number certificate corresponding to (16.24). The `sqrt 3 * Psi * epsilon` relative perturbation theorem is proved from that certificate, but the exact displayed `P^{-1}` operator-norm construction of `Psi` is still open.
- The (16.29) practical-bound theorems assume visible certificates: a left inverse `Pinv * sylvesterVecCoeff = 1`, an entrywise inverse bound `|Pinv| <= PinvAbs`, and an `IsSylvesterComputedResidualBudget` certificate. The residual-budget certificate can now be produced from an explicit residual error matrix `dR` with `Rhat = R + dR` and `|dR| <= Ru`; the floating-point proof producing that `dR` and the LAPACK estimator are not claimed.
- No new global axioms, `sorry`, `admit`, `unsafe`, or opaque placeholders are introduced by the Chapter 16 companion module.
