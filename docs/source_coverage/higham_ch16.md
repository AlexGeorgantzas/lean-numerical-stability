## Hidden-Hypothesis Summary

- The existing proved square theorems assume explicit Frobenius-norm bounds, `SepLowerBound`, nonzero norm-squared hypotheses where needed, and supplied linearized equations. These are domain/reused-theorem assumptions, not replacements for the target equation.
- `sylvesterSepInf` records the exact infimum object for `sep(A,B)`; `SepLowerBound` is now equivalent to a positive lower bound of that infimum when the feasible ratio set is nonempty, and the feasible set is proved nonempty in positive dimension. Rows needing an attained minimum remain open until minimizer existence is proved.
- `sylvesterBackwardErrorInf` records the backward-error eta quantity as an infimum of nonnegative feasible `IsBackwardError` certificates. The two-sided xi bridge and least-value attainment are proved for this infimum model under the stated positive-weight and nonempty-feasible-set hypotheses.
- `lyapunovBackwardErrorInf` records the Lyapunov-specific eta quantity as an infimum over structured certificates with one tied `DeltaA` perturbation and symmetric `DeltaC`. The two-sided Lyapunov eta/xi bridge and least-value attainment are proved under the stated positive-weight and nonempty-feasible-set hypotheses, with the symmetric-spectral optimizer discharging nonemptiness.
- `sylvester_schur_transform_identity`, `sylvester_schur_transform_solution_iff`, `existsUnique_isSylvesterSolutionRect_schurDiagonal`, `sylvesterVecCoeff_schurDiagonal_det_ne_zero`, `existsUnique_isSylvesterSolutionRect_schurTriangular`, and the `sylvesterVecCoeff_schurTriangular_*` vectorized coefficient theorems assume supplied factorizations and orthogonality; the triangular theorems also assume a supplied upper-triangular factor and nonsingular shifted column coefficients. They are not proofs of Schur decomposition existence, quasi-triangular block solvability, Hessenberg-Schur reductions, or floating-point stability.
- `SylvesterPsiFirstOrderBound` is an explicit condition-number certificate corresponding to (16.24). The `sqrt 3 * Psi * epsilon` relative perturbation theorem is proved from that certificate, but the exact displayed `P^{-1}` operator-norm construction of `Psi` is still open.

The supplied Schur-triangular wrappers discharge the left-inverse and entrywise inverse-bound certificates from the proved determinant-nonsingularity theorem for supplied orthogonal triangular factors; they still assume the Schur data, shifted diagonal nonsingularity conditions, and a computed-residual certificate/error model. They do not prove Schur existence, rounded Schur residual arithmetic, quasi-triangular block handling, or the LAPACK estimator.
