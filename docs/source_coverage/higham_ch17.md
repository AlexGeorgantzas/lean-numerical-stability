## Hidden-Hypothesis and Weak-Component Summary

- The legacy `ComputedIteration` sign convention is intentionally not presented as identical to source equation (17.1). The new `SourceComputedIteration` wrapper records the source sign and bridges by negating the local error term.
- `sourceComputedIteration_finite_sum` is exact algebra from the source-sign recurrence plus an explicit left-inverse certificate for `M`; it does not assume convergence, a floating-point model, the local-error bound (17.2), or the target error recurrence (17.5).
- `stationary_solution_finite_sum` is exact algebra: its final theorem assumptions are the splitting certificate and `Ax=b`; it does not assume convergence, a floating-point model, or the target finite-sum conclusion.
- `sourceComputedIteration_error_finite_sum` is exact algebra from `one_step_error_source`; it assumes the source-sign iteration model, splitting certificate, and exact solution equation, but does not assume convergence, an infinite series, or any forward-error bound.
- `stationaryScaledIterMatrix_similarity` assumes reciprocal diagonal scaling data and proves an exact similarity identity for the scaled iteration matrix; `stationaryScaledIterMatrix_charpoly_eq` then proves equality of characteristic polynomials using `Matrix.charpoly_mul_comm`. These theorems do not introduce spectral assumptions and do not export a separate eigenvalue-multiset theorem.
- `singular_stationary_iterate_finite_sum` is exact algebra for (17.21): it specializes the source-sign recurrence to zero local error and needs only the left-inverse certificate for `M`, not nonsingularity of `A`, semiconvergence, a Drazin inverse, or a limiting argument.
- `singular_consistent_second_term_telescope` and `singular_stationary_iterate_consistent_split` are finite algebraic consequences of consistency and the Neumann telescoping identity. They do not assume or prove the existence of `lim G^m`, semiconvergence, an index-one decomposition, or a Drazin inverse.
- `singular_error_split_finite` is the finite algebraic core of (17.27). It defines and uses `singularErrorSourceTerm` for (17.28). The Drazin-specialized wrapper `singular_error_split_finite_of_indexOneDrazin_projector` now constructs `E = (I-G)(I-G)^D` from an explicit `IndexOneDrazinInverse` certificate and proves the required fixed-null hypothesis. The surrounding projector algebra now proves the complementary zero-products, fixed-projector idempotence, finite-power invariance `G^k(I-E)=I-E`, range-projector commutation `G^kE=EG^k`, and powered range sandwiching `E(G^kE)=G^kE`, but it still does not prove existence of the Drazin certificate from semiconvergence/Jordan data or take a limiting argument.
- `singularErrorSourceTerm_norm_bound_of_local_error` proves the finite normwise (17.29) surface by deriving the source `gamma_x` budget through `local_error_normwise_simplified`, then applying the uniform-budget normwise `S_m` theorem. It is still finite; the convergence of the infinite sums used later in (17.30)-(17.32) remains open.
- `singularErrorSourceTerm_componentwise_bound_of_local_error` proves the finite componentwise (17.29) surface from the existing local-error model and componentwise iterate-growth predicate. It is also finite and does not claim the later singular-system limiting bounds.
- `residual_finite_sum` is exact algebra from `one_step_residual`; it assumes the splitting certificate, exact solution equation, and legacy computed-iteration sign convention, but does not assume convergence, a local-error bound, or any residual norm estimate.
- `normwise_residual_sigma_finite_bound` is a finite sigma-form normwise wrapper over `residual_finite_sum`; it controls a finite sum of norm products and is separate from the source's matrix-valued sigma.
- `finiteResidualSigma` names the source-facing finite partial matrix `sum |H^k(I-H)|`; `residualSigmaTsumMatrix` and `residualSigmaTsum` name the literal entrywise `tsum` matrix series and its infinity norm. The HasSum wrappers instantiate the `tsum` surface from concrete entrywise sums, `residualSigmaTsum_le_of_row_sum_le` exposes the row-sum route to a scalar bound, and `residualSigmaTsum_le_diagonalizable_max_bound_direct` proves the displayed (17.20) maximum bound directly for the literal `tsum` sigma under concrete real diagonalization data and `|lambda_i| < 1`. The series theorem `residualSigmaTsum_le_diagonalizable_max_bound_of_infNorm_bound` gives an alternative q-certified bridge through the finite-partial supremum envelope.
- `stopping_test_rhs_backward_subordinate`, `stopping_test_matrix_backward_subordinate`, and `stopping_test_mixed_backward_subordinate` close the subordinate-norm statements (17.33a)-(17.33c). They reuse the Chapter 7 normwise backward-error theorem for the matrix and mixed cases and do not claim the componentwise absolute-value variants mentioned after (17.33).
- `stopping_test_rhs_backward_componentwise`, `stopping_test_matrix_backward_componentwise`, and `stopping_test_mixed_backward_componentwise` close the real-valued componentwise absolute-value variants stated after (17.33). The matrix-only wrapper uses zero right-hand-side budget in Oettli-Prager and proves the produced `Δb` is zero; these wrappers are real API surfaces, not complex componentwise-norm statements.

Their bound wrappers require bounded-above ratio sets; the componentwise `theta_x` wrapper also makes the source zero-component caveat explicit via `hzero` rather than modeling an extended-real infinity value.
