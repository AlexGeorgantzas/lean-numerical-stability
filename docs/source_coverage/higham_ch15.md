## Honest-strength notes

- **Lemma 15.2 (abstract + concrete).** The chain (b) is *derived* in `lemma152b` from a bundle (`PNormPair`) whose seven fields are exactly Higham's printed dual-norm/Hölder/operator primitives — `dualp(v)ᵀv=‖v‖_p`, `‖dualp(v)‖_q≤1` (weaker than the printed `=1`, so the theorem is stronger), `dualq(w)ᵀw=‖w‖_q`, `‖dualq(w)‖_p=1`, Hölder `uᵀv≤‖u‖_q‖v‖_p`, and `‖Av‖_p≤‖A‖_p‖v‖_p`. The conclusion is never a hypothesis. Non-vacuity is established by two fully-discharged concrete instances: `pNormPair_two` (p=2, `opP = opNorm2 A` the Mathlib spectral operator norm; Cauchy-Schwarz as Hölder) and `pNormPair_one` (p=1, `opP = oneNorm A` the max-column-sum; `dualp=signVec`, `dualq=±e_j`). Hence `gammaSeq_two_le_opNorm2` and `gammaSeq_one_le_oneNorm` are genuine lower bounds on the true operator norms.
- **Page 291 precise convergence prose (audited 2026-07-21).** The scalar sentence is now an actual topological result: `gammaSeq_tendsto_ciSup` derives `Tendsto γₖ atTop (𝓝 (⨆k,γₖ))` solely from the existing monotonicity and operator-norm bound, and `exists_gammaSeq_limit` records the source bounds on the limit. The vector-subsequence sentence is closed abstractly under the missing topological property `IsCompact {x | pN x = 1}` and unconditionally for the concrete Euclidean instance using the repository's proved compact sphere.
- **`DEFER-UNDEFINED-SOURCE`: stationary limit.** The source-internal sentence “if Algorithm 15.1 converges, it converges to a stationary point when `1<p<∞`” is not citation-dependent, but the printed algorithm permits set-valued dual choices at zero without specifying a single-valued selection, continuity, or the zero-case domain of the stationary quotient. Those omissions prevent one unambiguous theorem at the printed generality. The precise bridge `xseq_limit_is_fixed_of_continuousAt` adds exactly continuity of `xnext` at the limit; `eq15_3_gradient_two_eq_zero_of_fixed` and `xseq_two_limit_is_stationary_of_continuousAt` prove the faithful qualified correction for the concrete smooth case `p=2` with `Ax̄≠0`, the differentiability condition already used by equation (15.3).
- **Endpoint termination and source dimension audit.** `pNormPair_one_terminates_by_n_plus_one` proves the `p=1` bound by showing every post-update estimate is one of `n` column norms. A new fully concrete symmetric `pNormPair_inf` (`pN=‖·‖∞`, `qN=‖·‖₁`, extreme dual maps, `opP=‖A‖∞`) supports `pNormPair_inf_terminates_by_n_plus_one`, whose pigeonhole invariant is the strictly increasing 1-norm of the selected row. Higham introduced Algorithm 15.1 for rectangular `A ∈ ℝ^{m×n}`: the same `p=∞` proof counts `m` row vertices and therefore gives `m+1`, while the printed paragraph says `n+1` for both endpoints. The repository interface is square, so both formal endpoints literally have the displayed `n+1` bound; the rectangular mismatch is recorded rather than hidden.
- **Rank-one special case — qualified closure and printed discrepancy.** `opNorm2_rankOne_eq` proves `‖uvᵀ‖₂=‖u‖₂‖v‖₂`. If `u≠0` and `vᵀx₀≠0`, `rankOne_two_converges_second_step_of_pairing_ne_zero` proves that the first updated estimate equals this exact norm and the second test succeeds. The phrase “whatever `x₀`” is not valid uniformly under Higham's set-valued definition at zero: `rankOne_second_step_whatever_x0_unit_opNorm_counterexample` packages the permitted total choice `dualp(0)=e₀`, the nonzero factor defining `A=e₁e₁ᵀ`, the unit start `x₀=e₀`, and the strict failure of the first updated estimate to equal `‖A‖₂`; the supporting recurrence lemmas show that every iterate stays at `e₀` and every estimate is zero. This is classified as a source-level dual-selection discrepancy, not silently weakened.
- **`DEFER-EXTERNAL` — Boyd [155, 1974].** The statement that a strong local maximum with no zero components attracts the iterates linearly is explicitly attributed to Boyd and is not derived in Chapter 15. It is citation-dependent and outside the local source proof surface, so it is recorded as `DEFER-EXTERNAL`.
- **`DEFER-EXTERNAL` — nonnegative irreducible global convergence.** The statement that nonnegative `A`, irreducible `AᵀA`, `1<p<∞`, and positive `x₀` imply `xₖ→x` and `γₖ→‖A‖ₚ` is also explicitly attributed to Boyd [155]. Its Perron–Frobenius/global-dynamics proof is not present in the chapter or the local formal interface, so it is recorded as `DEFER-EXTERNAL`, not as an omitted or assumed theorem.
- **Algorithm 15.3.** The repository recursion stores `γ` from the *previous* iterate on the converged branch, so its stored `.γ` field is not in general `‖A·(returned x)‖₁`. We therefore report, exactly as the algorithm prescribes, `γ := ‖Ax‖₁` for the genuine final iterate, and prove the new invariant `H15_Algorithm15_3_x_oneNorm_eq_one` (`‖x‖₁ = 1` for the start `n⁻¹e` and every basis vertex `eⱼ`), upgrading the existing `≤1` invariant to equality.
