## Honest-strength notes

- **Lemma 15.2 (abstract + concrete).** The chain (b) is *derived* in `lemma152b` from a bundle (`PNormPair`) whose seven fields are exactly Higham's printed dual-norm/Hölder/operator primitives — `dualp(v)ᵀv=‖v‖_p`, `‖dualp(v)‖_q≤1` (weaker than the printed `=1`, so the theorem is stronger), `dualq(w)ᵀw=‖w‖_q`, `‖dualq(w)‖_p=1`, Hölder `uᵀv≤‖u‖_q‖v‖_p`, and `‖Av‖_p≤‖A‖_p‖v‖_p`. The conclusion is never a hypothesis. Non-vacuity is established by two fully-discharged concrete instances: `pNormPair_two` (p=2, `opP = opNorm2 A` the Mathlib spectral operator norm; Cauchy-Schwarz as Hölder) and `pNormPair_one` (p=1, `opP = oneNorm A` the max-column-sum; `dualp=signVec`, `dualq=±e_j`). Hence `gammaSeq_two_le_opNorm2` and `gammaSeq_one_le_oneNorm` are genuine lower bounds on the true operator norms.
- **Algorithm 15.3.** The repository recursion stores `γ` from the *previous* iterate on the converged branch, so its stored `.γ` field is not in general `‖A·(returned x)‖₁`. We therefore report, exactly as the algorithm prescribes, `γ := ‖Ax‖₁` for the genuine final iterate, and prove the new invariant `H15_Algorithm15_3_x_oneNorm_eq_one` (`‖x‖₁ = 1` for the start `n⁻¹e` and every basis vertex `eⱼ`), upgrading the existing `≤1` invariant to equality. Both printed conclusions then hold at full strength with no added hypothesis.
- **Algorithm 15.4 (CLOSED at printed strength, 2026-07-18).** `H15_Algorithm15_3_stored_gamma_realized` proves that the bounded power-method state's stored scalar is `‖Aw‖₁` at some normalized iterate `‖w‖₁=1`; this is deliberately an existential provenance theorem because a nonconverged step replaces the state's `x` field after computing its scalar. `H15_Algorithm15_4_exact_ratio_witness` then case-splits on the estimator's actual `max`: for the power arm it uses that normalized provenance witness, and for the alternating arm it uses `w=lapackAltVec`. In both cases it constructs `v=Aw` and proves the literal equality `‖v‖₁/‖w‖₁=γ`, together with `γ≤‖A‖₁`. The older `_ratio_witness` is now derived as a weaker compatibility corollary.
- **Algorithm 15.5.** `Uy=d` with `dⱼ=±1` is *proved* (`linpackYSteps_solves`), not assumed, via the upper-triangular row split and a frozen-coordinate stability lemma; the sign rule `linpackSign` is the book's weighted look-ahead test. The nonzero-diagonal side condition is *derived* from nonsingularity (`det U = ∏ᵢ Uᵢᵢ`), so the headline hypotheses are the printed ones. The lower bound is unconditional; `w ≥ 0` is carried for fidelity though the bound holds for all `w` (documented).
- **Theorem 15.6 (CLOSED at printed strength — 2026-07-18).** `Ch15DixonProbability.lean` derives a rotation-invariant small-ball estimate directly from normalized standard Gaussian direction: it bounds a one-dimensional Gaussian density by `399/1000`, integrates the conditional strip, controls the tail norm by its second moment, and sharpens the result to the printed `4/5` coefficient. `Ch15DixonClosure.lean` then produces a unit top eigenvector of the actual Gram matrix `BᵀB`, proves `λmax(BᵀB)=‖B‖₂²`, proves the all-`k` lower spectral component and the all-`k` deterministic upper inequality, and converts the strip bound into the success probability `≥ 1 - 0.8 θ^{-k/2} √n`. The public endpoint `higham15_6_dixon_closed` also derives `BᵀB=(AAᵀ)⁻¹` from the supplied two-sided inverse certificate; it has no marginal-law, tail-bound, spectral, or target-bearing premise.
- **Theorem 15.7 (VERIFIED at printed strength — closed 2026-07).** The base `tridiag_exact_inv_abs`/`Ch15.H15_Theorem15_7` assumed the sign-coherence hypothesis `hSignCoherent` (every inverse-factor product `U_inv i k · L_inv k j ≥ 0`), which is target-equivalent to the conclusion AND false when pivots are negative (e.g. `U⁻¹₀₀·L⁻¹₀₀ = 1/u₀ < 0` for `u₀<0`). `Ch15Closure.H15_Theorem15_7_of_absLU_eq` now closes 15.7 from ONLY the printed `|L||U|=|A|` (plus tridiagonal LU structure, nonzero pivots, and the standard bidiagonal inverse-entry formulas that merely pin `U⁻¹`/`L⁻¹`). The correct crux is **common sign** (not each ≥0): for fixed `(i,j)` the terms `tₖ := U⁻¹_{ik}L⁻¹_{kj}` satisfy `U_{l+1,l+1}·t_{l+1} = (U_{l,l+1}·L_{l+1,l})·t_l` (derived from the inverse formulas), and the diagonal of `|L||U|=|A|` gives `0 ≤ U_{ii}(L_{i,i-1}U_{i-1,i})`, so consecutive terms share a sign ⟹ `|∑ₖtₖ|=∑ₖ|tₖ|` (`c15_abs_sum_of_pairwise_nonneg`). Axiom-clean `[propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound]`. *(This both corrects a prior CLOSED/VERIFIED overclaim on the base and genuinely closes it.)*
- **Theorem 15.8 (VERIFIED at printed strength — corrected 2026-07).** `Ch15Closure.H15_Theorem15_8_of_rowDiagDominant` takes the PRINTED `IsRowDiagDominant n A` and DERIVES the row-sum crux `∑ₗ∑ₖ|U⁻¹||U| ≤ 2n−1` internally: the LU recurrence propagates row diagonal dominance to `|U_{i,i+1}| ≤ |U_{ii}|` (`c15_tridiag_rowDom_U_super_le_diag` → `c15_bidiag_U_isDiagDominantUpper`), whence the bidiagonal-inverse magnitude bound and `c15_bidiag_rowSum_bound` give the constant; then the base `tridiag_diagdom_cond_bound` applies. The `(2n−1)` constant is in the conclusion (not smuggled); the `hU_inv_eq`/`hU_inv_ut` hypotheses only pin `U_inv` to the (unique) bidiagonal inverse, independent of dominance. *(This corrects the prior inaccurate "row-sum bound proved unconditionally in `unit_bidiag_row_sum_bound`" note — that base lemma itself assumes `|V|≤1`, `|V⁻¹|≤1`.)* Axiom-clean `[propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound]`.
- **Theorem 15.9 (CLOSED at printed strength — 2026-07-18).** `Ch15IkebeClosure.H15_Theorem15_9_of_irreducible_rightInverse` replaces the older formula-assuming wrapper with the direct Ikebe recurrence argument. For the upper triangle, the last inverse column is proved to have nonzero top entry: a zero top entry propagates through every homogeneous leading row using the nonzero superdiagonal, contradicting the final diagonal equation of `A A⁻¹=I`. Every column `j` satisfies the same homogeneous recurrence above row `j`, so cross multiplication with the last column yields the upper rank-one representation. A finite square right inverse is also a left inverse; transposing that identity and reusing the same argument with the nonzero subdiagonal yields the lower representation. The only endpoint hypotheses are `0<n`, the repository's literal `IsIrreducibleTridiag T`, and `IsRightInverse n (tridiag_to_matrix T) A_inv`. No LU factorization, nonzero-pivot premise, inverse-entry formula, or target-bearing rank premise is assumed.

## Residuals / obstructions

- No selected Chapter 15 obligation remains open. The former Theorem 15.6 obstruction was removed without needing a separately packaged Beta marginal: normalized independent Gaussians plus rotational invariance give the required sphere-coordinate strip estimate directly.
- **Lemma 15.2 general real p (documented residual).** The abstract `PNormPair` tier proves Lemma 15.2 for *any* `p` once the dual-norm data is supplied; the two endpoint instances Higham singles out (p=2 → power method on `AᵀA`; p=1 → LAPACK basis) are fully built. A third instance for general `p ∈ (1,∞)` is not built because Mathlib lacks a packaged mixed-`p` vector dual norm with its induced operator `p`-norm and attained `dualp`. This residual is only the general-`p` `dualp` construction, not any part of Lemma 15.2's logic.
